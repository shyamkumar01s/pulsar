About

Bajaj Pulsar NS160 is a street bike available in 2 variants and 4 colours. The Bajaj Pulsar NS160 is powered by 160.3cc BS6 engine which develops a power of 17.03 bhp and a torque of 14.6 Nm. With both front and rear disc brakes, Bajaj Pulsar NS160 comes up with anti-locking braking system. This Pulsar NS160 bike weighs 152 kg and has a fuel tank capacity of 12 liters.

Bajaj Pulsar NS 160 Overview

Design

The Bajaj Pulsar NS160 sports a sharp and aggressive design inspired by the NS200. It features bold tank shrouds, muscular bodywork, and a split seat layout. The sporty rear grab rails, underbelly exhaust, and dual-tone color schemes give it a premium look in the 160cc segment.

Features

The NS160 comes equipped with a halogen headlamp with pilot lamps, LED tail lamp, and a semi-digital instrument console. It also features clip-on handlebars, perimeter frame for better stability, and dual disc brakes with single-channel ABS for enhanced safety.

Riding Posture & Comfort

The NS160 offers a sporty yet comfortable riding posture. With slightly rear-set footpegs and a forward-leaning stance, it strikes a balance between everyday comfort and performance-oriented ergonomics. The split seat is well-cushioned for both rider and pillion.

Engine, Mileage & Performance

The Pulsar NS160 is powered by a 160.3cc oil-cooled, single-cylinder engine that delivers 17.2 PS at 9000 rpm and 14.6 Nm at 7250 rpm. It comes paired with a 5-speed gearbox. The bike offers brisk acceleration and a top speed of around 120 kmph, with a mileage of approximately 45â€“50 kmpl.

Handling & Ride Quality

Thanks to its perimeter frame and well-tuned suspension, the NS160 delivers excellent handling and stability. It feels confident in corners and remains composed at high speeds. The telescopic front forks and nitrox monoshock at the rear provide a good balance of comfort and performance.

Should You Buy Bajaj Pulsar NS160?

If you're looking for a performance-oriented commuter that looks aggressive, handles well, and offers good value, the Bajaj Pulsar NS160 is a great pick. It's suitable for both daily rides and occasional weekend fun, making it a versatile option in the 160cc category.
