Bajaj Pulsar 180 Overview

Design

The Bajaj Pulsar 180 maintains the muscular and aggressive design language of the Pulsar series. It features a sculpted fuel tank, sharp tank shrouds, a sporty bikini fairing, and signature twin-strip LED tail lamps.

Features

It comes equipped with a semi-digital instrument cluster, halogen headlamp with twin pilot lamps, LED tail light, split grab rails, and alloy wheels. The bike also includes an electric start and engine kill switch.

Riding Posture & Comfort

The Pulsar 180 offers a slightly aggressive yet comfortable riding posture. The seat is long and well-padded, making it suitable for city commutes as well as short highway trips. The ergonomics strike a balance between sportiness and comfort.

Engine, Mileage & Performance

Powered by a 178.6cc air-cooled, single-cylinder engine, the Pulsar 180 delivers 17 PS of power and 14.2 Nm of torque. It is paired with a 5-speed gearbox. The bike offers a decent fuel efficiency of around 45–50 kmpl, considering its performance.

Handling & Ride Quality

With a telescopic front fork and twin rear gas-charged shock absorbers, the Pulsar 180 handles city traffic and rough roads with ease. It feels planted at higher speeds and offers confident braking with its front disc and rear drum setup.

Should You Buy Bajaj Pulsar 180?

The Pulsar 180 is ideal for those looking for a powerful yet practical commuter with a sporty edge. It delivers reliable performance, classic Pulsar styling, and good comfort, making it a solid choice in the 180cc segment.
